Welcome and Overview

What the planner is, who it's for, and how to access it.

30-second version

A lesson planner built for Dio teachers. Open it in your browser, fill in your timetable once, and you're done. No login, no account, nothing to install. Your notes stay on your device. Use it every day or dip in whenever you need to plan ahead.

What is this?

A lesson planner built specifically for Dio. It runs entirely in your browser — no logins, no accounts, no apps to install. Open it, set up your classes and timetable once, and you've got a planning tool that understands our rotating 7-day cycle, our terms, our holidays, and our Day 0 events.

It was built by Victor Canela, a colleague who wanted something better than spreadsheets.

Who is it for?

Any teacher, any subject, any year level. If you have a timetable at Dio, this is for you.

How to access it

Open this link in your browser:

vcanela.github.io/lesson-planner

That's it. Bookmark it. Everything runs on your device.

Adding it to your home screen

You can save it to your home screen so it opens like a standalone app and works even without internet.

On a laptop (Chrome or Edge):

  1. Open the link above
  2. Look for the install icon in the address bar (a small monitor with a down arrow, or a "+" symbol)
  3. Click it and confirm

On an iPhone or iPad (Safari):

  1. Open the link in Safari
  2. Tap the Share button (the square with an arrow pointing up)
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Tap "Add"

On an Android phone (Chrome):

  1. Open the link in Chrome
  2. Tap the three-dot menu in the top right
  3. Tap "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App"
  4. Confirm

What's already set up for you

You do not need to configure any of the following:

  • School: Dio
  • Cycle: 7-day rotation
  • Term dates: All four terms for 2026
  • Statutory holidays: Auckland Anniversary, Waitangi Day, Good Friday, Easter Monday, ANZAC Day, King's Birthday, Matariki, Labour Day
  • Day 0 dates: Orientation (27-28 Jan), Athletics Day (17 Feb), EOTC Week (23-27 Feb), Teacher Only Days (15 May, 31 Aug), House Music (1 Jul), Final Days (7-8 Dec)
  • Anchor date: Already set so the cycle aligns correctly

If any of these change during the year, you can update them yourself in the Setup tab (see Customising Setup).

Getting Started

Setup takes about five minutes. You only do this once.

Open the planner and tap the Setup tab along the top.

Step 1 — Add your classes

Scroll down to the Classes section and click "+ Add Class" for each class you teach.

For each class, enter:

  • Code — the short code you know it by (e.g. 9SCI3, 12PHY1)
  • Description (optional) — something to remind you what it is (e.g. "Year 9 Science, Period 3 start")
  • Colour — pick one from the palette; each class gets its own colour so you can spot it at a glance

You can reorder your classes using the up/down arrows.

Step 2 — Build your timetable

Scroll to the Timetable section. You'll see a grid with days across the top (Day 1 through Day 7) and periods down the side (P1 through P6).

  • Click a cell and select one of your classes from the dropdown, or type the class code directly
  • For periods where you don't have a class, type nc (non-contact)

Step 3 — Choose your display mode

  • Light — light background, easy on the eyes
  • Dark — dark background, good for evening planning
  • Light Bold — stronger contrast on a light background
  • Dark Bold — stronger contrast on a dark background

You can change this any time.

Step 4 — Choose a theme (optional)

This only affects the Planning Lab — a motivational tracker. It has zero impact on how the planner works.

  • Physics 🔬
  • Coffee ☕
  • Cooking 🍳
  • Ocean 🌊
  • NZ Birds 🐦

Step 5 — Save

Scroll to the bottom of the Setup page and click Save Configuration.

You're done. Head to the Week tab and start planning.

Core Views

Four views, each showing your planning from a different angle. You'll probably spend most of your time in Week View.

📅 Term View
A full term laid out as a calendar grid.
Best for: seeing the whole picture at once, spotting planning gaps.
🗓️ Week View
Your Monday-to-Friday timetable for the week.
Best for: everyday planning and quick edits across the week.
📋 Day View
Full detail for a single day, period by period.
Best for: detailed lesson planning, adding notes and resources.
📚 Class View
Every lesson for one class, listed in order.
Best for: checking your sequence and planning across a term.

Term View 📅

A bird's-eye look at an entire term. Each day appears as a small tile in a calendar grid.

What you can see at a glance:

  • Which day of the cycle each date falls on
  • Day 0 events (highlighted with their label)
  • Holidays and weekends (greyed out)
  • The mid-morning type for each day (Tutor, Dean's Assembly, etc.)
  • A small fraction on each tile (e.g. 3/5) showing how many teaching periods you've planned vs how many you have that day
  • Coloured dots across the top of each tile representing your P1-P6 classes for that day

You can switch between terms using the buttons at the top. Clicking a day opens it in Day View. Clicking a week number (on the left) jumps to that week in Week View.

Week View 🗓️

This is your main workspace. It shows Monday to Friday with your full timetable laid out in a grid. Each column shows the date, the cycle day number (D3, D5, etc.), and the mid-morning type. Each cell shows the class code, the topic if entered, a flag icon if your notes contain a flag word, and a green dot if the period has been planned.

Clicking any cell opens a quick-edit box at the bottom of the screen where you can type a topic and press Enter.

Clicking "Full detail" in the quick-edit box takes you to Day View for that specific period.

Other things you can do in Week View:

  • Day notes row: a small text area per day for meetings, reminders, or duty notes
  • Class spotlighting: click any class badge above the grid to highlight where that class appears during the week — a popup shows the exact periods and times
  • Mid-morning setup panel: expand this at the bottom to set the mid-morning type for each day of the week at once
  • Navigate: use the left/right arrows to move between weeks, or hit "Today" to jump back to the current week
  • Export: at the bottom, download the full week or just your reflections as a text file

Day View 📋

The most detailed view. Shows every period of a single day in order, including break times. For each teaching period you'll see the class code, start time, and period label.

Clicking a period expands it. You can choose:

  • Quick mode — a single text field for the topic. Good for a one-liner.
  • Detailed mode — three fields: Topic/Subject, Notes and Tasks, and Resources and Links.

At the top of the expanded period, a lesson context strip tells you the lesson number for that class this term (e.g. "Lesson 12/48"), and the dates of the previous and next lessons with that class.

Other features:

  • Day notes: a text area at the top for meetings or reminders
  • Mid-morning block: choose the type for that day — this adjusts break labels and timings accordingly
  • Reflections panel: expandable section at the bottom for end-of-day thoughts
  • Day 0 dates: the timetable disappears and you get a simple label and notes field instead
  • Print: a print button gives a clean printout of the day with navigation hidden

Class View 📚

Shows every lesson for a single class in a list. Click a class badge at the top to select which class you're viewing. Filter by term or view all at once.

Each lesson shows the date, period, cycle day, topic (or "Click to plan" if empty), and lesson number. The current day's lesson is highlighted as TODAY; the next upcoming one is labelled NEXT.

Clicking a row opens a quick-edit box (same as Week View). Click "Full detail" to jump to Day View.

Tip

Class View is particularly good for checking your long-term sequence, spotting gaps, and seeing how many lessons you have left in the term.

Planning Your Lessons

What you type, where, and the small features designed to make it quicker.

Quick mode vs Detailed mode

When you expand a period in Day View, you'll see two tabs:

  • Quick — a single text field. Type your topic and move on. This is all you need most of the time.
  • Detailed — three separate fields:
    • Topic / Subject — what the lesson is about
    • Notes and Tasks — your lesson plan, activities, instructions
    • Resources and Links — URLs, file names, handout references

You can switch between modes at any time. Anything you type in Quick mode appears in the Topic field if you switch to Detailed, and vice versa. Nothing is lost.

Non-contact periods only show a single notes field — useful for jotting what you're using that time for (marking, meetings, prep).

Quick editing from Week View and Class View

You don't have to open Day View every time. In both Week View and Class View, clicking a period or lesson opens a small edit box where you can type a topic and press Enter. It saves immediately. Click "Full detail" if you need the full set of fields.

Day notes

At the top of each day (in both Day View and Week View) there's a text area for things that aren't tied to a specific period — staff meetings, duty, reminders, anything you want to see when you look at that day.

Mid-morning block

Each day has a mid-morning block type that controls the labels and timings for the two slots between P2 and P3:

  • Tutor (T) — Tutor Time + Morning Tea
  • Dean's Assembly (DA) — Dean's Assembly + Morning Tea
  • Full School Assembly (FSA) — Full School Assembly + Morning Tea (note: this shifts all period times slightly to accommodate the longer assembly)
  • Chapel (C) — Morning Tea + Chapel

Set this per day in Day View, or for the whole week at once using the "Mid-morning setup" panel at the bottom of Week View.

Reflections

At the bottom of Day View there's an expandable Reflections panel — a space for end-of-day thoughts about what went well and what you'd change. Reflections are optional, but if you use them:

  • A small dot appears next to the Reflections heading on days that have content, so you can tell at a glance
  • You can export all your reflections for the week as a text file from Week View

Flag words

If you type any of the following words in your topic or notes, the planner will mark that period with a small flag icon so it stands out:

todo prepare tbc tbd check remind follow up incomplete print photocopy

Flagged items appear in a summary list at the bottom of Week View. Click the small "flags" label at any time to see the full list.

Lesson context

When you expand a period in Day View, a small strip tells you the lesson number for that class this term (e.g. "Lesson 12/48 this term"), the date of the previous lesson with that class, and the date of the next lesson. Good for continuity planning.

Saving

Tip

Everything saves automatically. When you type in a field and click away, your work is saved to your browser within a moment. There is no save button to press during normal planning. Just type and move on.

The Planning Lab

Entirely optional. Skip it if it's not your thing — it has zero impact on how the planner works.

The Planning Lab is a motivational tracker. As you plan your lessons, it quietly keeps score in the background and rewards you with experience points, levels, and achievements. Access it from the Lab tab.

How it works

  • You earn 10 XP for every lesson you plan
  • You earn 50 XP for a perfect week (every teaching period planned)
  • You earn bonus XP when you unlock achievements

As your XP grows, you level up through a series of titles. Your current level and XP are shown in a small strip at the top of most views.

Streaks

If you plan at least 80% of your lessons in a week, that counts towards a streak. The streak number appears as a small fire icon 🔥 next to your level. It resets if you drop below 80% in a week.

Achievements

There are ten achievements to unlock — things like planning your first lesson, completing a full week, reaching 50% of the term, and so on. Each one has a short description and a bit of flavour text. Hover over the badge icons to see what they are.

Themes

The Lab comes in five visual themes. Each one changes the achievement names, level titles, and the weekly progress illustration:

  • Physics 🔬 — launch from the pad to orbit
  • Coffee ☕ — from green bean to cafe owner
  • Cooking 🍳 — from sous chef to iron chef
  • Ocean 🌊 — from the shore to the abyss
  • NZ Birds 🐦 — from the forest floor to soaring above the Alps

Change your theme any time in the Setup tab. It only affects the Lab — nothing else changes.

Weekly visual

A vertical fill bar tracks your planning progress for the current week. Milestone markers sit at their percentage positions; the bar fills upward as you plan more periods. Just a nice thing to glance at.

Exporting and Sharing Your Plans

A few ways to get your plans out of the app and into other formats.

Export a full week

At the bottom of Week View, click "Full week" to download a text file containing everything for that week: each day's date, cycle day, and mid-morning type; day notes; every period with its class, topic, notes, and resources; and any reflections you've written.

Export reflections

If you've written reflections during the week, a "Reflections" button appears in the export panel. This downloads just your reflections — useful for keeping a teaching journal or sharing with a mentor or appraiser. The button only appears if at least one day that week has a reflection written.

Copy to clipboard

Alongside the download button there's a Copy button, so you can paste your week plan directly into an email, Teams message, or anywhere else.

What can you do with the exported file?

The exported file is structured plain text. You can:

  • Read it as-is in any text editor
  • Paste it into a document, email, or chat
  • Open it in Word, Google Docs, or Notion — the formatting carries across
  • Feed it into an AI tool (like ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude) and ask it to summarise your week, draft a report, suggest improvements, or create resources based on your plan
Tip

That last point is the real power — your planning data comes out in a clean, structured format that AI tools work very well with.

Print a day

In Day View, a small print button near the top navigation uses your browser's built-in print function and hides all navigation and buttons, so you get a clean printout of just that day's plan. Handy for sticking on your desk or handing to a reliever.

Your Data

Where your data lives, how to back it up, and how to sync across devices.

How it all works

Why there's nothing to install

The planner is a webpage, but your browser treats it like a proper app — it caches all the files locally so it works offline, and it can be added to your home screen. You get the same experience as a downloaded app without ever installing anything. Your browser handled it the moment you first opened the link.

What "browser storage" means

Every modern browser has a small private storage area for each website — like a notepad that belongs to that site, kept on your device. When you type a topic and click away, the planner writes it there. It's private, instant, and works without internet. The catch: it only exists in the browser you used, on that device.

What a Gist is

A Gist is a private named file stored on GitHub's servers — think of it as a shared clipboard in the cloud that only you can see. When you enable sync, the planner pushes your data to that file and other devices pull from it. GitHub is a well-established, reputable service. Your data isn't going anywhere ad-hoc.

Be aware

Because your data lives in the browser: switching to a different browser (e.g. Chrome to Firefox), clearing your browser data, or using a private/incognito window will mean your data is not there. Back up regularly — it takes about ten seconds.

Backing up your data

  1. Click the small save icon (💾) next to the view tabs at the top
  2. In the panel that opens, click "Download Backup"
  3. A file will download to your device — keep it somewhere safe

To restore from a backup:

  1. Open the same panel
  2. Click "Choose File" and select your backup file
  3. Review the summary (it shows how many notes and settings were found)
  4. Click "Confirm" to import

Importing merges the backup into your current data. It will overwrite any overlapping entries.

Cloud sync (optional)

If you want your data to stay in sync across multiple devices, you can connect the planner to a GitHub Gist. This requires a free GitHub account and a personal access token.

Not sure what that means?

Skip this section. Regular backups work perfectly well. Sync is for people who regularly switch between a school laptop and a personal device and want changes to appear on both without manually transferring files.

If you'd like to set it up:

  1. Go to GitHub and create a free account if you don't have one
  2. Create a personal access token with "gist" permission (there's a direct link in the sync panel)
  3. Open the save panel (💾) in the planner
  4. Paste your token and leave the Gist ID blank
  5. Click "Connect" — the planner will create a private Gist for you
  6. From then on, use "Push" to upload your data and "Pull" to download it on another device

A small coloured dot on the save icon shows your sync status: green = connected, pulsing = syncing, red = something went wrong.

The planner also auto-pushes changes a few seconds after you edit something, so in most cases you won't need to push manually.

Updates to the planner itself

From time to time the planner may be updated with improvements or fixes. This happens automatically — the next time you open the app, your browser fetches the latest version. Your planning data is never affected by updates.

Customising the Setup

Most of the setup is already done for you (see Welcome). This section is for when something changes during the year.

All changes are made in the Setup tab. Remember to scroll down and click Save Configuration after making any changes.

Editing term dates

If a term start or end date changes:

  1. Find the term in the Terms section
  2. Click the start or end date to open the calendar picker
  3. Select the new date

You can also add or remove entire terms using the "+ Add Term" button or the small ✕ on each term card. Terms are automatically renumbered.

Adding or editing holidays

If a new public holiday is announced or a date changes:

  1. Scroll to the Statutory Holidays section
  2. Click dates on the calendar to toggle them on or off
  3. Give each holiday a name in the text field that appears

Holidays automatically become Day 0 dates as well — you don't need to add them in both places.

Adding or editing Day 0 dates

If the school adds a new Day 0 event (teacher only day, sports day, special event):

  1. Scroll to the Day 0 Dates section
  2. Click the date on the calendar
  3. Type a label (e.g. "Teacher Only Day", "Swimming Sports")

Dates that came from statutory holidays appear with a lock icon 🔒 — you can't remove them here. Remove the holiday first.

Changing the anchor date

The anchor date tells the planner which cycle day a specific date falls on, and it calculates everything else from there. It's already set correctly for 2026.

You would only need to change this if the school resets the cycle mid-year (rare), or you're setting up for a new year.

  1. Scroll to the Anchor section
  2. Pick a date you're certain about (e.g. the first Monday of a term)
  3. Select which cycle day that date is

If you're unsure, check with your timetabler or the daily notices.

Editing classes

To add a new class, click "+ Add Class" in the Classes section, enter the code, description, and colour. To edit, change the fields directly. To remove, click ✕ on its card — this does not delete any lesson notes already written for that class. To reorder, use the up/down arrows.

Editing the timetable

If your timetable changes (e.g. a class swap, a new semester): click the cell you need to change in the Timetable section, then select a class, type a class code, or type nc for non-contact. Changing the timetable does not delete existing notes — it only changes what class the planner expects in that slot going forward.

Changing the cycle length

If you ever need to change the number of days in the rotation, use the Cycle Length dropdown near the top of the Setup tab. Be aware this will affect the timetable grid and you may need to re-enter some cells.

Tips and Common Questions

Answers to the things people usually ask, and a quick glossary at the bottom.

Are there keyboard shortcuts?

In any quick-edit box (Week View and Class View):

  • Enter — save and close the edit box
  • Escape — cancel and close without saving

In text fields throughout the app, your work saves automatically when you click away. No need to press anything.

What is "weekend mode"?

If you open the planner on a Saturday or Sunday, a small "Weekend mode" label appears next to your name at the top. The planner still works normally — it just acknowledges you're planning on your own time. The "Today" button in Week View will jump to the coming Monday instead.

What if I'm teaching a class that isn't on my regular timetable?

You can still write notes for that period — just open it in Day View and use it normally. The class badge will show whatever's assigned in your timetable for that slot, but your notes are tied to the date and period, not the class code.

Can two teachers share the same planner?

Not directly. Each person's data is stored separately in their own browser. Two teachers using the same link will each have their own independent planner.

To share a plan with a colleague (e.g. for team planning or handing over to a reliever), use the export feature in Week View to download a text file and send it to them.

My data disappeared — what happened?

The most common causes:

  • You opened the planner in a different browser than usual — each browser has its own storage
  • You (or IT) cleared your browser data — this deletes local storage along with cookies and cache
  • You're in a private or incognito window — these don't keep data after closing

If you have a backup file, you can restore everything using the import feature in the save panel (💾). This is why regular backups are worth doing.

Can I use this on my phone or tablet?

Yes. The planner works in any modern browser on any device. The layout adapts to smaller screens, though the Week View grid is most comfortable on a laptop or tablet in landscape.

For the best experience on a phone or tablet, add it to your home screen (see Welcome) so it opens full-screen without browser toolbars.

Does it work offline?

Yes, once the page has loaded. If you lose internet during the day, you can keep planning normally. Your data saves to your device regardless of connection. If you have cloud sync set up, it will push your changes next time you're back online.

Does the planner update automatically?

Yes. The next time you open it — in your browser or from your home screen — it will quietly pick up the latest version. Your planning data is never affected by updates.

I have a suggestion or found a problem

Talk to Victor. He's friendly and likes feedback.

Glossary

Anchor date
A known date-and-cycle-day pair that the planner uses to calculate the cycle day for every other date. Think of it as the reference point for the rotation.
Browser storage
A small private storage area inside your browser where the planner saves your notes. Each browser on each device has its own separate storage.
Cycle day
The day number in the rotating timetable (Day 1 through Day 7). This advances by one for each teaching day and pauses on weekends, holidays, and Day 0 events.
Day 0
A school day with no regular timetable. Could be a public holiday, teacher only day, sports day, or special event. The cycle pauses on these days.
Flag words
Specific words (like todo, tbc, print) that the planner recognises and highlights with a flag icon so they stand out as action items.
Gist
A private named file stored on GitHub's servers. The planner uses it as a bridge between devices when cloud sync is enabled.
Non-contact (nc)
A timetable period where you don't have a class. The planner still lets you write notes for these slots.
